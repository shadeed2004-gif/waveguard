\chapter{Performance Evaluation}
\label{chap:evaluation}

\section{Testing Methodology}

Performance evaluation was conducted in three phases:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Bench testing} – All modules individually validated on a breadboard
        before integration.
  \item \textbf{Pool testing} – Assembled buoy floated in a controlled 2~m×4~m pool;
        wave conditions simulated by hand at varying intensities.
  \item \textbf{Near-shore testing} – Prototype deployed at $\approx$200~m offshore
        in Kanayannur (10.05\textdegree{}N, 76.18\textdegree{}E) on a calm day
        (April 2026) and during mild swell.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Accelerometer Accuracy}

Post-calibration stationary readings showed $a_{\rm rms} = 0.004 \pm 0.003$~g,
well below the 0.03~g \textsc{calm/moderate} boundary.
Comparison with a reference USB accelerometer (ADXL345) showed agreement within
$\pm$0.008~g across the $0$--0.15~g range relevant to Kallakkadal conditions.

\begin{equation}
  \bar{I} = 0.5 \times 150 + 0.5 \times 80 +

Measured battery runtime on a 2\,200~mAh cell: \textbf{6.4~hours} (pool test),
\textbf{6.1~hours} (near-shore test with frequent SMS events).

\subsection{SMS Reliability}

In 50 triggered SMS events across all test phases, 48 messages were successfully
delivered (96\% delivery rate).
The two failures occurred when the SIM800L was not registered to the network
(CREG = 0), indicating a momentary coverage gap at the near-shore test site.

No mis-classification was observed in pool tests with deliberately controlled
motion; offshore conditions produced only CALM and lower MODERATE readings during
the calm-day test, consistent with a 0.3~m measured wave height.
