\chapter{Literature Survey}
\label{chap:litsurvey}

\section{Introduction}

This chapter reviews prior work in three interconnected domains relevant to WaveGuard V4: (i) coastal ocean monitoring and early-warning systems, (ii) IoT-based environmental sensing platforms, and (iii) data fusion techniques combining in-situ and satellite data sources.

\section{Coastal Ocean Monitoring and Early-Warning Systems}

\subsection{Traditional Warning Approaches}

Early coastal warning infrastructure relied primarily on tide gauge networks and offshore wave-rider buoys maintained by national agencies.
Rabinovich and Stephenson \cite{rabinovich2004} demonstrated the effectiveness of bottom-pressure recorders in detecting trans-oceanic tsunamis, establishing the precedent for real-time deep-sea monitoring.
However, such systems require expensive mooring infrastructure and have historically focused on deep-water events rather than near-shore swell amplification.

Stopa and Cheung \cite{stopa2014} analysed long-period swell propagation across ocean basins using numerical models and showed that Indian Ocean storm systems can generate dangerous near-shore swells at the Kerala coastline 48–72 hours after generation.
Their work highlights the critical window during which an effective warning system must operate.

\subsection{Low-Cost Buoy Systems}

The proliferation of embedded systems has enabled the development of low-cost buoy platforms.
Casas-Prat and Sierra \cite{casas2013} reviewed multiple prototype buoy designs based on GPS and accelerometer integration, noting that RMS vertical acceleration provides a reliable proxy for significant wave height in moderate sea states (Hs \(< 3\) m).
This finding directly motivates the use of the MPU-6050 IMU in WaveGuard V4.

Raghukumar et al.\ \cite{raghukumar2019} evaluated a low-cost Spotter buoy and reported significant wave height measurement errors of less than 10\% compared to offshore wave rider buoys, validating the commercial viability of compact inertial sensing.

\subsection{Kallakkadal-Specific Research}

Srinivas et al.\ \cite{srinivas2016} conducted a case study of the 2011 Kallakkadal event along the Kerala coast, attributing the surge to a remote Southern Ocean storm system generating 18–22 second-period swells.
They identified the lack of near-shore detection capability as the primary factor in the 10 fatalities recorded and recommended deployment of shallow-water wave gauges along the Ernakulam coast — the exact gap addressed by WaveGuard V4.

\section{IoT-Based Environmental Monitoring}

\subsection{General IoT Architectures for Environmental Sensing}

Gubbi et al.\ \cite{gubbi2013} proposed a comprehensive IoT vision for smart environments, establishing the three-layer model (perception, network, application) that has since become canonical in sensor network design.
WaveGuard V4 maps directly onto this model: the ESP32 buoy constitutes the perception layer, the WiFi/HTTP pipeline constitutes the network layer, and the FastAPI + React stack constitutes the application layer.

Atzori et al.\ \cite{atzori2010} surveyed IoT application domains and specifically highlighted disaster management as a high-impact use case requiring sub-second sensor-to-dashboard latency — a target met by the WaveGuard V4 prototype.

\subsection{ESP32 in Environmental Applications}

The ESP32 system-on-chip by Espressif Systems has been widely adopted in environmental monitoring owing to its integrated dual-core processor, hardware floating-point unit, and native WiFi/Bluetooth connectivity.
Kumar et al.\ \cite{kumar2021} demonstrated an ESP32-based water quality monitoring station achieving 99.2\% uptime over a six-month deployment in a coastal lagoon, confirming the platform's suitability for marine environments.

\subsection{MPU-6050 for Wave Sensing}

The InvenSense MPU-6050 six-axis IMU has been used in wave-sensing applications due to its low cost (\$1–2 USD) and sufficient sensitivity for ocean swell detection.
Herbers et al.\ \cite{herbers2012} validated the use of MEMS accelerometers for directional wave spectrum estimation, showing good agreement with pitch-roll buoy measurements for periods above 6 seconds.
The MPU-6050 samples at up to 1 kHz, which adequately resolves the 8–22 second swell periods characteristic of Kallakkadal events.

\section{Data Fusion and Alert Classification}

\subsection{Sensor Fusion Principles}

Sensor fusion combines data from multiple heterogeneous sources to produce estimates with higher accuracy and reliability than any individual sensor.
Hall and Llinas \cite{hall1997} introduced the widely cited JDL data fusion model, which categorises fusion into five levels from signal-level combination to cognitive refinement.
The WaveGuard V4 fusion algorithm operates at JDL Level~1 (object assessment), combining buoy accelerometer metrics with satellite wave height to classify sea state.

\subsection{Multi-Threshold Classification}

Rogers et al.\ \cite{rogers2005} proposed multi-threshold classification matrices for coastal hazard assessment, demonstrating that dual-variable thresholds reduce false alarm rates by up to 40\% compared to single-variable systems.
This principle is implemented in WaveGuard V4's three-tier alert matrix (Table~\ref{tab:alert_matrix} in Chapter~\ref{chap:design}).

\subsection{Integration of Satellite Data}

The Open-Meteo API provides forecast-quality significant wave height (SWH) derived from ECMWF ERA5 reanalysis and operational NWP models \cite{openmeteo2022}.
Bauer et al.\ \cite{bauer2015} reviewed ECMWF's Integrated Forecasting System and demonstrated SWH forecast skill of approximately 0.9 correlation with buoy measurements at 48-hour lead times, providing a reliable complementary signal for WaveGuard V4.

\section{Web Frameworks for Real-Time Dashboards}

FastAPI \cite{fastapi2023} has emerged as the leading Python framework for high-performance REST and WebSocket services due to its ASGI architecture and automatic OpenAPI schema generation.
Tiangolo \cite{tiangolo2019} demonstrated that FastAPI achieves throughput exceeding 45,000 requests per second on commodity hardware — adequate for the sub-second polling model of WaveGuard V4.

React \cite{react2023}, maintained by Meta, provides a component-based declarative UI model ideal for real-time dashboards where partial state updates must re-render efficiently via its virtual DOM diffing algorithm.

\section{Summary}

The literature review establishes that:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Near-shore swell detection via low-cost IMU buoys is technically validated.
  \item IoT architectures using ESP32 achieve adequate reliability for coastal deployment.
  \item Dual-variable fusion significantly reduces false alarm rates over single-sensor systems.
  \item Satellite NWP data (Open-Meteo) provides sufficiently accurate SWH for real-time fusion.
  \item FastAPI and React constitute a performance-adequate technology stack for this application.
\end{itemize}

The identified research gaps — absence of an integrated, publicly accessible, bilingual near-shore warning system for Kerala's Kanayannur coast — directly motivate the WaveGuard V4 design presented in subsequent chapters.
