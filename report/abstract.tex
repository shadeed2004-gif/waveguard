\chapter*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}

Coastal communities in Kerala, particularly those around Kanayannur, face recurring
threats from \textit{Kallakkadal}---a destructive long-period swell surge that arrives
without visible storm activity and claims lives every monsoon season.
Existing forecasting methods rely on satellite observations and meteorological models that
provide insufficient resolution and lead-time for localised near-shore zones.

WaveGuard~V4 is a low-cost, autonomous, IoT-based buoy system designed to provide
real-time early warning of hazardous wave conditions.
The system integrates an ESP32 microcontroller (240~MHz, 520~KB RAM) with an MPU6050
tri-axial accelerometer (16\,384~LSB/g, 50~Hz sampling) and a NEO-6M GPS module
to continuously measure and geo-locate buoy motion offshore.
Three alert tiers are defined: \textsc{calm} ($<0.03$~g), \textsc{moderate}
(0.03--0.075~g), and \textsc{high-wave} ($>0.075$~g).
On threshold breach, the system simultaneously broadcasts a WiFi dashboard update
and dispatches an SMS alert via a SIM800L GSM module within 8--12~seconds.
A 18650 Li-ion cell (2\,200~mAh) powers the system for 6--8~hours of continuous
operation, with idle draw of 80~mA and active draw of 150~mA.
The FastAPI backend persists all sensor readings in SQLite and serves a React-based
real-time dashboard.
Prototype validation at the Kanayannur coastal zone (10.05\textdegree{}N,
76.18\textdegree{}E) demonstrated sub-100~ms sensor response, accurate tier
classification, and reliable GSM delivery, confirming viability as an affordable
community-level early-warning solution.

\vspace{6pt}
\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} IoT, coastal monitoring, wave detection, ESP32, MPU6050,
SIM800L, Kallakkadal, early warning system, FastAPI, real-time dashboard.
